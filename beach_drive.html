Project Overview

I found the place on Redfin. In under a week I went from doing a drive-by of the property, to having it and septic inspected, to closing on October 24, 2025.

DB was my broker, and as a thank-you, he did 2 trailers of dump runs. The first was taken the morning I met him there. He had 2 of his employees come to help load up the trailer, and it took no time grabbing stuff from the garage and ripping carpet out from the bedroom.

It actually went a little faster than I would have chosen. I was inclined to sort through what might be worth keeping. In hindsight, I'm glad I didn't have that choice. The pile was disgusting, and it was great to get a big jump on clearing out what turned out to be a massive rat nest.

The next two days I pulled debris from the garage and scraped the concrete floor of the compacted sludgy base of a mature rats nest. I uncovered rats both dead and alive, bird bones, and the like. Especially disgusting was a broken but live fridge that had to be defrosted then hauled off. I'd have to take fresh air breaks when my gag reflex started sending warning shots.

Breaks consisted of pulling out incredible shoots of blackberry that dangled down through the grape vine gazebo, and ivy making its way into the backyard from beyond the fence. The landscaping was clearly at one point well maintained, and diverse, but now fallen into neglect. I didn't take ample photos of or comprehensively identify plants before leaves fell off for the winter, but I did note that there are many interesting specimens.

I prioritized cleaning out the garage to take advantage of the second dump run ASAP, and try to stop the place from being a major vermin haven. I staged a trailer full in the driveway, then one morning it was magically gone when I got there...a real gift. Thanks again, DB.

I did a run in the tiny truck with just the dead fridge and hazmat.

After that, back to the home itself, the first priorities were actually olfactory: it had to stop smelling so foul ASAP. I sealed up rat ingress points, started drying out smelly stains on the subfloor so I could seal it up with paint, and tracked down other sources of stink.

The kitchen sink's drainage was super clogged (several utensils had fallen in), and the faucet was impractical for the sink. I cleaned the drains out, replaced the drain cups, and the faucet.

There were holes drilled along the deck-facing side of the gutter which acted like a sprinkler to pour water onto the carpeted deck. The gutter was integrated into the structure of the awning in such a way it's very difficult to clean out. This was one of the last things I did before leaving town for a couple of weeks.

I also left a 1.2A fan running, working on drying out the moist section of the living room subfloor.
